## **Bylaws**

## **Of  Lacamas Watershed Council**

**PREAMBLE**

The Lacamas Watershed Council (“LWC”) was founded in 2020 by Camas citizens concerned about frequent algae blooms in the Camas lakes, and was established as a nonprofit organization in 2021\. The LWC was formed to drive water quality improvement in the Lacamas Creek Watershed streams and lakes. The group advocates 1\) for improved water quality based on the best available science and 2\) for the safety, health and recreational values of the Lacamas Creek Watershed, with a focus on the lakes within the City of Camas. We believe this can be best achieved through positive collaboration with all identified stakeholders.

###### **ARTICLE 1\. MEMBERSHIP**

**1.1	No Members**  
The corporation shall initially have no members.  The corporation shall have supporters, friends and affiliates. 

**1.2	Membership**  
Membership classes, the manner of election or appointment of members, the qualifications and rights of each class of members, and dues for members may be established by amendment to these Bylaws. All members may have an active role within the organization. 

**ARTICLE 2\. BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

**2.1.**  	**General Powers**  
The corporate powers, property and affairs of the Corporation shall be exercised, conducted, and controlled by the Board of Directors (hereinafter referred to the “Board”).  

**2.2.	Number**

The Board shall consist of  no less than 5 and no more than 15 Directors. The number of Directors may be changed from time to time by amendment to these Bylaws, provided that no decrease in the number shall have the effect of shortening the term of any incumbent Director.

**2.3 	Election of Directors** 

**2.3.1 	Initial Directors**  
The initial Directors named in the Articles of Incorporation shall serve until the first annual meeting of the Board. 

**2.3.2 	Successor Directors**  
Successor Directors shall be elected each year by the Board at its annual meeting. The Board shall consist of three classes of Directors, each class being based on the date of appointment, as nearly equal in number as possible. The Directors of each class shall be elected upon majority vote of the remaining two classes.

**2.4 	Term of Office**  
A Director shall hold office until the next annual meeting of the Board or until his or her successor is elected, whichever is later, barring death, resignation, or removal.  Directors may serve successive three-year terms without limit on the total number of terms served.

**2.5 	Resignation**  
Any Director may resign at any time by written notice to the President or the Secretary at the registered office of the corporation, or by giving oral or written notice at any meeting of the Directors.  Any such resignation shall take effect at the time specified therein, or if the time is not specified, upon delivery thereof and, unless otherwise specified therein, the acceptance of such resignation shall not be necessary to make it effective.

**2.6 	Removal**  
One or more Directors may be removed from office, with or without cause, by a majority vote plus one of the Board.    
**2.7   	Vacancies**  
A vacancy in the position of Director may be filled by the affirmative vote of a majority of the remaining Directors through less than a quorum of the Board, if a quorum is not possible due to the vacancy. A Director who fills a vacancy shall serve for the unexpired term of his or her predecessor in office.  
 

## **ARTICLE 3\.  MEETINGS OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

**3.1. 	Regular Meetings**  
A minimum of twelve (12) Board meetings shall be held in a 12 month period, one each month of the calendar year, at times and places selected by the President and set forth in appropriate meeting notices. By resolution, the Board may specify the date, time and place for holding regular meetings without other notice than such resolution. Meetings can be held in person or online.

**3.2	Annual Meeting**  
The meeting in February is designated as the annual meeting of the Board.

**3.3. 	Special Meetings**  
A special meeting may be called by the President of the Lacamas Watershed Council or any three members of the Board of the Directors.

**3.4. 	Notice of Meetings**  
Notice of Board meetings stating the time, date and purpose of each shall be made to each Director no less than 10 days prior to the date fixed with the exception of special meetings or meetings noticed by prior resolution. 

**3.5	Notice of Special Meetings**  
Special meetings shall be scheduled based on the availability of Board members. Notice for these meetings should be as flexible as possible. 

**3.6**  	**Quorum**  
For conducting all business, including amendment of the Bylaws or the Articles of Incorporation, a quorum is necessary.  A quorum shall consist of a majority of the Board members plus one. If a quorum is not present at a meeting, a majority of the Directors present may adjourn the meeting from time to time without further notice.

## **3.7**	**Manner of Acting**

An act of the majority of the Directors present at a meeting at which there is a quorum shall be the act of the Board, unless the vote of a greater number is required by these Bylaws, the Articles of Incorporation or applicable Washington law.

## **3.8**	**Action by Board Without a Meeting**

Any action which could be taken at a meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting if a consent in the form of a record, which clearly sets forth the action to be taken, is executed by all the Directors. Any such record shall be inserted in the electronic minute book as if it were the minutes of a Board meeting. For purposes of this Section 3.8, record means information inscribed on a tangible medium or contained in an electronic transmission.  

**3.9	Meetings by Telephone/Online**  
Members of the Board or any committee designated by the Board may participate in a meeting of such Board or committee by means of a conference telephone call or similar communications equipment by means of which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other at the same time.  Participation by such means shall constitute presence in person at a meeting.

**3.10	Compensation**  
Members of the Board shall not receive any fee for their services as members.  The Board may, at its discretion, reimburse individual Directors for costs and expenses incurred fulfilling their obligations as Directors.

## **ARTICLE 4\. OFFICERS**

**4.1.	Number and Qualifications**  
The officers of the corporation shall be a President and Chief Spokesperson, Vice President of Strategic Planning and Communications, Vice President of Operations, Secretary and Information Officer, Treasurer, and such other officers as the Board may see fit,  each of whom shall be elected by the Board. All officers must be Directors of the corporation.

**4.2	Election and Term of Office**  
The officers of the corporation shall be elected each year by the Board at the annual meeting of the Board. Unless an officer dies, resigns, becomes incapacitated or is removed from office, he or she shall hold office until the next annual meeting of the Board or until his or her successor is elected. The same person may not hold more than one office. Officers may be re-elected three times, for a maximum of 3 years in any one position. At that time, the incumbent must serve in an at-large position for a minimum of one year before eligibility for service as an Officer again. The incumbent can then stand for election to the same or a different office if invited to do so, with renewal for a total of three additional years. 

**4.3**	**Resignation**  
Any officer may resign his or her position at any time by delivering written notice to the President, the Vice President, the Secretary or the Board, or by giving oral or written notice at any meeting of the Board.  Any such resignation shall take effect at the time specified therein or, if the time is not specified, upon delivery thereof and, unless otherwise specified therein, the acceptance of such resignation shall not be necessary to make it effective. He or she may continue to serve as an at large member of the Board.

## **4.4**	**Removal**

Any officer or agent elected or appointed by the Board may be removed by the affirmative vote of at least a majority plus one of the Board whenever in its judgment the best interests of the corporation would be served thereby.  Removal can be corrected at any regular meeting or any special meeting called for that specific purpose.

## **4.5**	**Vacancies**

A vacancy in any office created by the death, incapacity, resignation, removal, disqualification, creation of a new office or any other cause may be filled by the Board for the unexpired portion of the term or for a new term established by the Board.

## **4.6**	**Compensation**

The officers shall receive no compensation for their service as officers but may receive reimbursement for reasonable expenditures incurred on behalf of the corporation.

#### **ARTICLE 5\. DUTIES OF OFFICERS**

**5.1.**	**President and Chief Spokesperson**   
He/she shall be the chief executive officer of the corporation, have general supervision of and give direction to the affairs of the Corporation, shall preside at all meetings of the Board as Chair, and shall be an ex officio member of all other committees. The President shall perform such other duties as the Board may require.

**5.2**  	**Vice President of Strategic Planning and Communications**   
He/She shall perform the duties of the President in the absence of or in case of incapacity of the President and shall perform such other duties as the Board may require.

**5.3**	**Vice President of Operations**   
He/She shall perform the duties of the President in the absence of or in case of incapacity of the President and Vice President of Strategic Planning and Communications and shall perform such other duties as the Board may require.

**5.4**	**Secretary and Information Officer**   
He/She shall: (a) keep any records of the proceedings of all meetings of the Board; (b) regularly monitor and conduct correspondences through the corporate email address; (c) manage corporate website; (d) update corporate Social Media  and notices in a timely manner; (e) and  perform all the usual duties of the office and such other duties as the Board may require.

**5.5**	**Treasurer**   
He/She shall (a) have charge and custody of and be responsible for all funds and securities of the corporation, in consultation and signature with another officer/Executive Director as specified annually; receive and give receipts for moneys due and payable to the corporation from any source whatsoever, and deposit all such moneys in the name of the corporation in banks, trust companies, fiscal sponsor or other depositories selected in accordance with the provisions of these Bylaws. He/She shall (b) submit to the Board at each annual meeting a report reviewing the operations of the Corporation for the previous year, and shall submit at other meetings of the Board such other reports as the Board may require.  The Treasurer shall (c) perform all the usual duties of the office, and such other duties as the Board may require.

**ARTICLE 6\. EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR**

The corporation may employ an Executive Director who shall be appointed, employed, and discharged by the Board.  If employed, the Executive Director shall manage the affairs of the corporation according to the policies, principles, practices and budget authorized by the Board, and shall be responsible for management of personnel, finances and programs.  If employed, the Executive Director shall be responsible for staff management including hiring, training, disciplinary action, and discharge.  If employed, the Executive Director shall serve as an ex-officio, non-voting member of the Board.  For the purpose of determining the number of Directors serving the corporation, the Executive Director shall not be considered a member of the Board.

#### **ARTICLE 7\. COMMITTEES**

**7.1**	**Executive Committee**  
The Board may appoint, from among its members, an Executive Committee of not less than five (5) or more than nine (9) members, to include the officers of the Board and the Chair of each of the Council’s standing Committees. The Board may also designate one or more of its members as alternates to serve as a member or members of the Executive Committee in the absence of a regular member or members.  The Board reserves to itself alone the power to change the membership of any committee, fill vacancies therein, and discharge any committee at any time. Subject to the foregoing limitations, the Executive Committee shall possess and exercise all other powers of the Board of Directors during the intervals between meetings.

**7.2**	**Other Committees**   
The Board may also appoint from among its members such other committees as they determine which shall consist of not less than two Directors and shall have such powers and duties as prescribed by the Board.  The President shall be an ex officio member of each committee.

**7.3**	**Rules of Procedure**  
A majority of the members of any committee may fix its own rules of procedure.  All action and planned action by any committee shall be reported by the Committee Chair, the Board liaison, or the Chair’s designee, to the Board at the meeting succeeding such action and shall be subject to revision, alteration, and/or approval by the Board.


**ARTICLE 8\. INTEREST OF DIRECTORS**   
**AND OFFICERS**

## **8.1**	**Conflict of Interest**

Directors and officers shall disclose to the Board any financial interest or personal relationship which the Director or officer directly or indirectly has or had in any person or entity which is a party to a transaction under consideration by the Board.  The interested Director or officer shall abstain from voting on the transaction.

**8.1.1**	A Director, member or employee should be sensitive to any interest he or she may have in a decision to be made by the Board of Directors, and, insofar as possible, recognize such interest prior to the discussion or presentation of the matter before the Board.

**8.1.2** When a Director, member or employee has a possible conflict of interests, the individual should disclose this information immediately to the President and Secretary. Disclosure should be made in writing or included in Board minutes.

**8.1.3** Upon disclosure, the Board should conduct a disinterested review of the matter. If holding a Board meeting is not practical, the Executive Committee – convened through a conference call if necessary – will handle the matter. The Board member who has the potential conflict of interests will not be present for the Board's or Executive Committee's discussion and vote, and thus, will not participate in resolution of the matter. The meeting minutes will indicate that the Director did not vote and was not present when the vote was taken.

**8.1.4** If a Director or member has a conflict of interest and does not disclose it to the Board, the Board may remove that Director/member from the Board/committee/position without notice. An employee may be fired.

**8.2**	**Review of Certain Transactions**  
Prior to entering into any compensation agreement, contract for goods or services, or any other transaction with any person who is in a position to exercise influence over the affairs of the corporation, the Board shall establish that the proposed transaction is reasonable when compared with a similarly-situated organization for functionally comparable positions, goods or services rendered.

# **ARTICLE 9\. ADMINISTRATIVE AND FINANCIAL PROVISIONS**

## **9.1**	**Loans**

No loans shall be contracted on behalf of the corporation and no evidence of indebtedness shall be issued in its name unless authorized by a unanimous resolution of the Board.  Such authority may be general or confined to specific instances.

## **9.2**	**Loans or Extensions of Credit to Officers and Directors**

No loans shall be made and no credit shall be extended by the corporation to its officers or Directors.

## **9.3**	**Checks, Drafts, Etc.**

All checks, drafts or other orders for the payment of money, notes or other evidences of indebtedness issued for more than $250 in the name of the corporation shall be signed by such officer or officers, or agent or agents, of the corporation and in such manner as is from time to time determined by resolution of the Board.  Any amount over $500 shall require two officer/agent signatures.

All deeds, mortgages, contracts, checks, notes and other obligations or evidence of indebtedness shall be signed in accordance with resolutions to be adopted by a majority vote of the Board.

## **9.4**	**Deposits**

All funds of the corporation not otherwise employed shall be deposited from time to time to the credit of the corporation in such banks, trust companies, fiscal sponsors’ accounts or other depositories as the Board may select.

## **9.5	Investments**

The Corporation shall have the right to retain all or any part of any securities or property acquired by it or donated to it in whatever manner and to invest and reinvest any funds held by it according to the judgment of the Board without being restricted to the class of investment.

## 

## **9.6**	**Books and Records**

The corporation shall keep at its principal or registered office copies of its current Articles of Incorporation and Bylaws; correct and adequate records of accounts and finances, minutes of the proceedings of its Boards and any minutes which may be maintained by committees of the Board; records of the names and post office addresses of its officers and Directors, and such other records as may be necessary or advisable.

## **9.7	Corporate Seal**

If the Board determines that it is advisable, the corporation shall have a corporate seal consisting of the name of the corporation, the state of its incorporation and the year of its incorporation.

## **9.8**	**Accounting Year**

Unless a different accounting year is at any time selected by the Board, the accounting year of the corporation shall be the twelve months ending December 31st.

## **9.9**	**Decision Making**

Strategic, tactical, and operational decisions are made by the Board of Directors, as well as all media-related decisions. Individual Committees will have their own methodology and report to the Board via their Chair. The exception is that all official organization communications must be approved by the Board of Directors, Executive Committee, or a delegated standing Committee BEFORE publication.

Our primary decision-making method is consensus of the Board of Directors; which is not unanimity but functionally defined as the willingness of members to support the decision publicly, regardless of their individual preference. 

Our secondary decision-making method is consultative by the LWC President after considering input from relevant stakeholders. This will be used when and if the Board or the Executive Committee cannot reach consensus or for decisions that need to be made quickly, are time-sensitive, or under circumstances where decision-making by consensus is not effective or practical. 

Decisions will be made by members in attendance and with the information available to them. If a member cannot attend a meeting, they should submit their feedback beforehand or provide it afterwards in a timely manner and based on available meeting notes.

#### **ARTICLE 10\. AMENDMENTS**

These Bylaws may be amended by any regular or special meeting of the Board called for that specific purpose by a vote of the majority of the Directors.

#### **ARTICLE 11\. WAIVER OF NOTICE**

Whenever any notice of a meeting is required to be given to any Director of this Corporation under Washington Law or these Bylaws, a waiver of notice in writing signed by the person or persons entitled to such notice is sufficient to satisfy the notice requirement for that meeting.  Such notice requirement shall also be deemed waived by any Board member attending the meeting who does not state his/her objection to a lack of notice at that meeting.  This notice requirement is also waived by any non-attending Board Member who does not state his/her objection to the lack of notice at the next properly-noticed Board meeting held after draft minutes/meeting records have been delivered in the same manner required for notice.  All such waivers shall be equivalent to  having received proper meeting notice.

#### **ARTICLE 12\. INDEMNIFICATION OF DIRECTORS, ADVISORS, AND OFFICERS**

Each Director and officer of the Corporation, and his or her heirs, executors and administrators, shall be indemnified by the Corporation against all costs, expenses and amounts of liability, including reasonable attorney’s fees, incurred or imposed in any action, suit, proceeding or claim in which the Director or officer may be made a party or otherwise involved.

This indemnification shall not apply with respect to any matter as to which such Director or officer shall be adjudged individually guilty of willful misfeasance or malfeasance in the performance of the duties as such Director or officer.  Further, this indemnification shall be made only with respect to any settlement and reimbursement which is for the best interests of the Corporation.  The foregoing writ of indemnification shall be in addition to and not exclusive of any and all other rights as which any such Director or officer may be entitled under any Bylaw, agreement, vote of Directors or otherwise.

**ARTICLE 14\. DISSOLUTION OF COUNCIL**  
In the event of dissolution of the Lacamas Watershed Council and after all obligations are satisfied, by a majority vote of its Board, all assets will be transferred to one or more 501(c)(3) organizations whose primary focus includes the ecological health and safety of all or part of the Lacamas Watershed.
