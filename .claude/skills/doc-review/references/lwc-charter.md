**Lacamas Watershed Council**

# **Charter**

#  

The Lacamas Watershed Council (LWC) was established by a group of Camas citizens concerned about frequent toxic algae blooms happening in Lacamas, Round and Fallen Leaf Lakes. They formed the LWC to drive improvement in the water quality of our beloved Lacamas watershed streams and lakes. LWC is an apolitical nonprofit citizen group that advocates for improved water quality based on the best available science. We believe this can be best achieved through positive collaboration with all identified stakeholders. 

**Mission Statement** [^1]

Our mission is to improve and advocate for the safety, health and recreational values of  the Lacamas Creek Watershed with a focus on the lakes within the City of Camas. Our efforts are aimed at researching and implementing best practice solutions to improve the water quality and wildlife habitats in our watershed and provide for the safe enjoyment of our streams and lakes.   
**Vision Statement**

The Lacamas Watershed’s streams and lakes are safe, healthy and enjoyable now with a robust lake management plan safeguarding the future.

**Strategic Objectives** 

The LWC strives to achieve its vision by:  
Exploring and helping resolve issues contributing to poor water quality in our streams and lakes; expediting action addressing safety and health concerns; and providing for long-term solutions to detect and quickly address changes in water quality within our watershed. 

Our efforts will include the following:

- Organizing and implementing projects to improve water quality alongside lake and governmental organizations, civic leaders and other strategic partners  
- Advocating for water quality in the governmental decision-making process  
- Updating the community on issues affecting Lacamas watershed streams and lakes  
- Educating citizens who live, work and play within our watershed

1. ### **Purpose**

   The LWC exists to sustain and preserve a healthful stream and lake environment within our watershed, enhancing wildlife habitat and the quality of aquatic recreation for Camas citizens. The LWC consists of lake lovers who live and enjoy Lacamas, Round, and Fallen Leaf lakes and our streams within the watershed.   
     
   Recognizing the jurisdictions of separate regulatory agencies and governmental entities associated with the watershed, the LWC functions as the primary advocate for water quality within the Lacamas Creek Watershed with a focus on the lakes within the City of Camas. The LWC also aims to function as an advisory resource supporting other groups, agencies, or contract entities regarding strategies to establish or improve stream and lake management policies and practices. The LWC is integral to the active management of water quality improvement efforts.

2. ##### **Guiding Principles (things we all hold to be true \- faith statements, how we do this work) and Tactical Goals ;** 

1. To achieve immediate improvement of water quality for the Lacamas Creek watershed by focusing on the following:

   1. Increasing awareness of the dangers of the current situation, 

   2. Shortening the time between toxin detection and toxin alert.  

   3. Improving the reach/scope of the toxin alert system. 

2. To research, collect, and analyze historical data regarding the water quality of the watershed with a focus on the lakes within the City of Camas.

3. To research, collect, and analyze best practices to improve the water quality of the watershed and Camas lakes for recreational purposes.

4. To support citizens, partners, and governmental agencies in their efforts to improve the water quality of the watershed with a focus on Camas lakes.

   1. To help identify and overcome hurdles to each project’s completion.

   2. In the short-term, help address hazardous conditions such as toxic algae blooms.

   3. In the long-term, help identify sources of excessive levels of nutrients and address associated eutrophication.

5. To organize efforts of the LWC to implement and complete water quality improvement projects.

6. To establish long-term technological and behavioral changes to prevent eutrophication and toxicity. 

7. To create strong ties with watershed partners as advocates for the best long-term water quality, with special emphasis on governmental agencies.

8. To help create long-term communication systems to update the community regarding the status of water quality issues with emphasis on Camas lakes.

9. To find innovative ways to incorporate watershed education into the community conversation.  

10. To strive for action that is evidence-based and founded on established best practices.

11. To improve water quality to a level that makes our streams and lakes a desirable place for all users.

12. To commit to effective collaboration and shared responsibility with all other groups seeking to improve water quality within our watershed, acknowledging that improving the safety and quality of our lakes and streams will only occur through the efforts of multiple groups and stakeholders.

13. To operate within legal and ethical organizational boundaries when sharing information.

3. ##### **Responsibilities**

1. Accountability to the members of the LWC and stakeholders in our projects/partnerships.  

2. Ensuring the use of and compliance with customary and usual governmental procedures, regulatory practices, and technical standards of water quality measurement.

3. Ignoring unwarranted political influence that is contrary to the goal of improving water quality within the watershed.

4. Ensuring an effective, ongoing program to measure, assist and improve water quality within the watershed.

5. Recommending priorities and resource allocation as it affects the improvement of water quality within the watershed.

6. Fostering collaboration between government entities and their constituents. 

7. Overseeing the effective utilization of financial and technical resources to promote collaboration among various stakeholders and effective engagement with community volunteers

   

4. ## **Membership**

Any concerned citizen who expresses a desire to participate can become a supporting member of the LWC. The goal is that all members may have an active role within our organization.

5. ## **Organization and Leadership Roles**

Board of Directors:  
The Board of Directors comprises the leadership of the LWC and is the voting membership of our organization. Participation as a member of the Board of Directors is at the invitation of and subject to a vote by its existing members. Each member of the Board plays a role in fulfilling the mission of the LWC and achieving our goals. Roles include the following:

* President & Spokesperson  
* Vice President of Strategic Planning and Communications  
* Vice President of Operations  
* Secretary & Information Officer  
* Treasurer   
* Committee Chairs

* #### 

For each of the Standing Committees listed below, the first order of business is to appoint a Chair and then create a Charter that outlines its purpose, membership, tasks and authority, to be approved by the Board of Directors. Each Chair will update the Board on a regular basis about the work of their respective committees.

* Executive Committee  
* Digital Communications Committee  
* Science and Research Committee  
* Fundraising Committee  
* Community Outreach and Action Committee

6. ## **Meetings of the Board of Directors and Standing Committees**

* LWC Supporters Meetings:  Will be called as determined by the Board of Directors when and as deemed necessary or prudent.

* Board of Directors Meetings: 

  * Regularly scheduled meetings will be held in person or virtually on a pre-agreed day and time.  

  * Regular attendance to Board and Committee meetings is expected by all members. Notification about inability to attend is considered a common courtesy.   
  * All Board and Committee members are expected to attend 75% of regularly scheduled meetings.   
  * Special meetings shall be scheduled based on the availability of Board or  Committee members and guest presenters.   
* Other Committees Meetings:   
  * Meetings will be held either in person or virtually as scheduled by the Chair of the respective committee.  

  * Notice of meetings and the meeting agenda shall be given at least one week but no less than 3 days prior to any meeting, unless time-sensitive.

7. **Decision-making**  
* Decision Level: Strategic, tactical, and operational decisions are made by the Board of Directors, as well as all media-related decisions. Individual Committees will have their own methodology and report to the Board via their Chair. The exception is that all official organization communications must be approved by the Board of Directors, Executive Committee, or a delegated standing Committee BEFORE publication.  
* Decision making process: Our primary decision-making method is consensus of the Board of Directors; which is not unanimity but functionally defined as the willingness of members to support the decision publicly, regardless of their individual preference. 

* Our secondary decision-making method is consultative by the LWC President after considering input from relevant stakeholders. This will be used when and if the Board or the Executive Committee cannot reach consensus or for decisions that need to be made quickly, are time-sensitive, or under circumstances where decision-making by consensus is not effective or practical. 

* Decisions will be made by members in attendance and with the information available to them. If a member cannot attend a meeting, they should submit their feedback beforehand or provide it afterwards in a timely manner and based on available meeting notes.


8. **Board of Directors Meetings \- Team Functions**   
* Agenda  
  * Agenda development is led by the President with input from all members.  
  * The Agenda will be provided to members in advance of meetings as is feasible. The President may add items as needed up to the time of the meeting. If needed and practical, the agenda may be changed by consensus at the meeting with approval of the President.

  * To the extent possible, informational topics will be limited in favor of time for LWC dialogue and decision-making.

* Note taking

  * Meeting notes are kept for each scheduled meeting, to establish a record of the knowledge gained at that meeting and to keep all members of the LWC informed. They are not true meeting “minutes” and do not need committee approval.  

  * Attendance is kept at all meetings of the Board and Executive Committee.

  * Primary note-taking for meetings is done by the Board’s Secretary.

  * In the Secretary’s absence, another member of the Board will volunteer or be appointed to take notes. 

  * Notes will be made available in the Google Drive folder for members of our Board of Directors and to the public upon request.  

  * Members and partners will be notified about LWC leadership’s progress, projects and other useful information through our monthly newsletters which will be sent out via emails and also published to our website to keep general membership and the public informed. 

* Authority to Access the LWC’s Google Drive Folder: “Lacamas Watershed Information”

  * All Board members have full editing access to our Google Drive and the documents within it. 

  * No member has permission to delete files in our Drive folders (with the exception of the President) unless determined as unnecessary or otherwise disposable by the Board of Directors. 

  * Only the President has authority to give permission to share documents from our Drive folders. Any requests for sharing must be approved by the President.  

  * For documents added to Drive folders, it is requested that they are notated in the Lacamas Watershed Council \- Table of Contents document.

  * It is the plan of the LWC to create a “viewing-only” folder for the public. 

* Quorum:  50% \+1 members of the Board of Directors or Executive Committee must be present in order to have a quorum.  

* Confidential Information \- Board and Executive Committee members are expected to exercise discretion in sharing information from or discussion at meetings of the committee. While most information will be publicly available, strategy and planning may require confidentiality.

* Roles  
  * Meeting Chair \- The Chair will be the LWC President or his/her designee.

  * Facilitator \-  The facilitator will ensure that the group norms are maintained. They will interrupt the group process if they observe norms being violated. This role will be fulfilled by members of the Committee on a rotating basis as assigned by the Chair.

  * Timekeeper \- The timekeeper will alert the group when the allotted time for discussion of an agenda item is coming to a close or at an end. This role will be fulfilled by members of the Committee on a rotating basis as assigned by the Chair.

  * Recorder \- Recorder will be the Secretary or an appointed notetaker (see above).

9. ##### **Authority**

   The LWC is a self-governing volunteer group consisting of local citizens.  LWC authority does not exceed that of any other citizen of Camas. LWC seeks to collaborate with, but is not accountable to, the Camas City Council or any other governmental entity, stakeholder, or strategic partner.    
     
   The Board of Directors has the authority to make all decisions for the LWC.  All other committees and members have only the authority granted to them by the Board in writing.   
   

10. ##### **Approvals**

    All needed approvals for spending, media communications, new projects, project assignments and completions must go through the Board of Directors.
